PRIORITY RESET CHECKLIST

Reset your focus when you feel overwhelmed and regain control

When everything feels urgent and you're not sure where to start, use this checklist to cut through the noise, identify what truly matters, and create a clear action plan.
Explore Related Categories
Manager Relationship & Leadership Style Confidence, Presence & Influence

When to Use This Checklist

  • When you have too many tasks and don't know where to start
  • After receiving conflicting priorities from multiple stakeholders
  • When you're feeling overwhelmed by your workload
  • At the start of a new week or quarter to reset focus
  • When you need to reprioritize due to changing circumstances

Pro Tips

  • Be ruthlessly honest about what can be dropped or delegated
  • Consider both importance and urgency when prioritizing
  • Share your priority reset with your manager for alignment
  • Schedule regular priority check-ins (weekly or bi-weekly)
  • Remember: saying "no" to some things means saying "yes" to what matters

Your Priority Reset Checklist

I have listed EVERYTHING currently on my plate (tasks, projects, commitments).
I have identified which tasks are truly urgent (time-sensitive) vs. important (high impact).
I have clarified what my manager's top 3 priorities are for me right now.
I have identified tasks that don't align with my core responsibilities or goals.
I have identified what I can stop doing, defer, or delegate without negative consequences.
I have communicated my proposed priorities to key stakeholders for feedback.
I have defined what "done" looks like for my top 3 priorities.
I have blocked dedicated focus time in my calendar for my most important work.
I have identified potential conflicts between priorities and planned how to address them.
I have established clear criteria for saying "yes" or "no" to new requests.
I have created a visual priority map (Eisenhower matrix or similar) for reference.
I have scheduled a follow-up check-in to review and adjust priorities as needed.
