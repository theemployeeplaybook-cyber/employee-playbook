CAREER ANCHORING FRAMEWORK

Anchor your career to what you actually want (and can defend)

Turn vague “next steps” into a clear anchor: what you’re building, what you won’t trade away, and how to choose roles that fit your strengths, energy and identity

Tip: aim for a clean anchor you can say out loud in one sentence. Then make every “next move” prove it.
How to use this framework

Work through each section once, fast and honest. Don’t aim for perfect wording — aim for an anchor you can defend. When you’re done, your output is: (1) your one-sentence anchor, (2) four non-negotiables, and (3) a decision filter that stops you saying yes to roles that dilute you.

Your anchor sentence Make it simple, defensible, and specific enough to guide choices.
0/4 complete
Write the anchor (one sentence)
What outcome do you want to be known for?
What problems are you willing to solve repeatedly?
What do you refuse to build your career on?
Note: vague anchors create drift. If your anchor can fit any job, it will guide none. Your anchor should exclude as much as it includes.
