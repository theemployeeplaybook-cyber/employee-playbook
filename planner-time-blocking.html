TIME BLOCKING TEMPLATE

Build a day that protects your priorities and makes “urgent” compete for space

Time blocking turns your calendar into a strategy tool. You decide what wins, when you do deep work, and where you’ll place admin, meetings, and buffers so your day doesn’t get hijacked.

Plan your blocks, then generate a clean daily plan and a boundary script you can use to defend it.
Related categories
Expectation, Burnout & Capacity

When to Use This

  • When your day gets eaten by other people’s urgency.
  • When you need protected focus blocks for high-value work.
  • When meetings are multiplying and deep work is disappearing.
  • When you’re doing “reactive work” all day and pushing priorities to evenings.
  • When you want a calmer day without losing output.
If it isn’t on the calendar, it gets sacrificed first.

How to Use It (fast)

  • Pick 1–3 real priorities. Everything else is “fit it in”.
  • Place deep work blocks first. Guard them like meetings.
  • Add buffers (15–30 mins) after meetings and before deadlines.
  • Batch admin into one block so it doesn’t leak into focus time.
  • Use the generated boundary script to push back politely.
